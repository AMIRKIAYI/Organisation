Transforming Lives: Our New Water Borehole in Marsabit

  • Water Projects, Community
  • 08 Comments

After six months of drilling and community engagement, we're proud to share the story of our newest water borehole in Marsabit County. This project now provides clean, reliable water to over 500 families in the Laisamis sub-county, reducing women's daily water collection time from 4 hours to just 15 minutes.

The journey began in early 2022 when community elders approached HOC about their water challenges. Women and children were walking up to 10km daily to fetch water from contaminated sources, leading to high rates of waterborne diseases and keeping children out of school. Our team conducted hydrogeological surveys and identified this location as having the most promising aquifer.

"Before this borehole, my daughters would miss school twice a week to help fetch water. Now they attend every day, and our family is healthier," shared Mama Asha, a mother of five from the community.

The technical installation involved:

  • Drilling to 180 meters depth
  • Installing a solar-powered pumping system
  • Building a 10,000-liter storage tank
  • Training a local water committee in maintenance

The project faced challenges when we hit unexpected rock formations at 120 meters, requiring specialized equipment. Thanks to our donors' flexibility, we overcame this hurdle and ultimately struck water at 165 meters - one of our most productive boreholes to date.

Community members celebrate at the borehole opening ceremony

Looking ahead, we're working with the community to establish vegetable gardens using drip irrigation from the borehole. This next phase will provide both nutrition and income opportunities for local families.
